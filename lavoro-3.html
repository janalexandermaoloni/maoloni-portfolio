Sole

Brand and web work for Sole DXB, the Middle East's leading street-culture festival. A bold, scalable visual language that works on a poster, a pass and a phone screen alike.

Client
Sole DXB
Role
Brand & web
Year
2026
Type
Festival identity

The project

A culture festival lives or dies by its identity. Sole DXB needed a visual language bold enough to own a city, posters, passes, stage screens, social, while staying unmistakably one brand from the first teaser to the final wristband.

The client

Sole DXB is an independent, community-driven festival and platform for youth and contemporary culture across the Middle East and North Africa. The audience is design-literate and demanding, which raises the bar on everything.

Technologies

  • Identity system: logo mark, type, colour and a flexible layout grid.
  • Web presence built in hand-written HTML, CSS and JavaScript.
  • Responsive, image-led pages with a fast, accessible front-end.
  • Asset kit designed to scale from a phone screen to a stage backdrop.

Creative process

We anchored the identity in one bold, confident mark and a tight palette, then stress-tested it across the messiest real-world formats, tiny avatars, huge banners, low-light stages. The web work translated that energy into a responsive site that feels like the festival, not a brochure about it.

Results

A cohesive, flexible brand that holds up everywhere it appears, paired with a site that turns curiosity into tickets. Loud when it needs to be, disciplined underneath.
