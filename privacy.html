Privacy & Terms

How this website handles your personal data, which cookies it uses, and the terms for using it.

Last updated: 1 July 2026

1. Data controller

This website is owned and operated by Alexander Maoloni (“Hexsole studio”). For any question about your personal data, write to alexmaoloni64@gmail.com.

2. What data we collect

We only collect the data you choose to give us:

  • Contact form data, name, surname, email address, optional phone number, subject and the content of your message.
  • Technical data, standard, non-identifying information your browser sends (such as browser type), used only to keep the site working correctly.

3. How we use your data

Information you send through the contact form is used solely to reply to your request and, if it leads to a project, to manage our working relationship. We never sell your data, and we do not use it for automated profiling.

4. Legal basis & retention

We process contact data on the basis of your explicit consent (the privacy checkbox) and our legitimate interest in answering enquiries. We keep messages only as long as needed to handle your request and any resulting project, after which they are deleted.

5. Your rights

Under the GDPR you can, at any time, request access to your data, its correction or deletion, or object to its processing. To exercise these rights, email alexmaoloni64@gmail.com and we'll respond promptly.

6. Cookies

This site is deliberately light on cookies. It does not use advertising or tracking cookies. We only use:

  • Local preferences, a value saved in your browser's localStorage to remember that you accepted the cookie notice and your light/dark theme choice. This never leaves your device.
  • Third-party embeds, the contact page embeds Google Maps, and pages load Google Fonts; these providers may set their own cookies. See their policies for details.

The cookie notice appears once on your first visit to the homepage and won't reappear after you accept it.

7. Third-party services

We rely on a few trusted providers: Google Fonts (typography) and Google Maps (the map on the contact page). These services process limited technical data under their own privacy policies.
